    Literary Analysis

    "The General" by Dispatch, in a literary sense, resembles more of a children’s story than an Anti-War song developed to bring to light the effects of war on families, friends and a soldier’s psyche. This childlike literary take further complements the overall thought process of a severely romanticized topic. This child like reverence can be seen in the first line, “There was a decorated general with a heart of gold/that likened him to all the stories he told” Through these lines it could be safe to assume that the narrator looks up to this general, a story telling “general with a heart of gold”. Within one of the general’s many tales is a story about a fight only he showed up for and not his soldiers. The reasoning behind his change of heart was a realization. His soldiers are humans, not machines for war, hence the “Shine your shoes/You got no time to lose/you are young men you must be living”.
